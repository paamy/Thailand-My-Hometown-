Top three activities to do at Bangkok

Wat Arun Ratchawararam

It is recognized as one of the most iconic and beautiful temples in Thailand. Located on the west bank of the Chao Phraya River, Wat Arun is famous for its magnificent 79-meter-high central spire, reputed as being Thailand's Eiffel Tower, which has a unique design and fine craftsmanship.

Chatuchak Market

Chatuchak Market sells many different kinds of goods, including plants, antiques, consumer electronics, cosmetics, pets, fresh and dry food and drinks, ceramics, furniture and home accessories, clothing, and books.

The Reclining Buddha

Wat Phra Chetuphon, is right behind the Temple of the Emerald Buddha – it’s a must-see for any first-time visitor to Bangkok. As one of the largest temple complexes in the city, it’s famed for its giant reclining Buddha that is 46 meters long, covered in gold leaf.
